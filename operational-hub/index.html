Sheet 05 / 08

Control logic — your staff stay in the loop

Fig 03 · 06 Rules R-01–R-06

Automation without judgment is a liability. The framework's most important drawings are the ones that decide when the AI must stop talking — written with your team in week one, rehearsed in week three, audited in week four.

RULES The handover rulebook
  1. R-01
    Human on request“Can I talk to someone?” ends the automation instantly — no persuasion, no friction.
  2. R-02
    Out of scope, out of handsAnything the playbook doesn't cover is escalated, never improvised.
  3. R-03
    Complaints go to peopleNegative sentiment or complaint language routes to a named team member with context.
  4. R-04
    Big deals get a personEnquiries above your value threshold are flagged for personal attention within the hour.
  5. R-05
    Compliance topics are off-limitsLegal, medical, financial-advice territory: captured, never answered by the agent.
  6. R-06
    Quiet hours honouredOvernight handovers queue with full context for the first person in — customers are told exactly when.

The rulebook is yours to change. Every rule is versioned in the sheet set and re-tested after every edit.
