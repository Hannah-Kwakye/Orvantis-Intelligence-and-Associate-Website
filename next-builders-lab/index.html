The method

Learn by shipping.

Tutorials evaporate. Shipped things stick. Every track runs the same three-beat loop, over and over, until building with AI feels like riding a bike.

1

Pick a real problem

Yours, your family’s, your business’s. If nobody would notice it being solved, it doesn’t make the cut. Real stakes make real builders.

2

Build it, together

Small groups, a coach at your elbow, AI doing the heavy lifting. You learn to direct the tools — brief them, doubt them, correct them — not just prompt and pray.

3

Ship it, show it

Every session ends with a demo. Applause is mandatory, polish is not. Then you pick the next problem — slightly bigger — and go again.

Fair questions

Before you ask…

Do I need to know how to code?

No. Most builders arrive never having written a line. You’ll leave understanding how software fits together and how to direct AI to write, fix and explain code for you. The coders among you just go deeper, faster.

Which AI tools do you teach?

Whatever ships your project best — today that means frontier chat assistants, coding agents, image and voice tools, and the boring-but-mighty automation platforms. Tools change monthly; the method doesn’t. We teach judgement, not menus.

What should I bring?

Professionals, founders and teams: your own laptop and one real problem you care about. Kids: just the kid — we provide laptops, accounts and snacks. Everyone: a healthy suspicion of anything that demos too smoothly.

Can I visit before committing?

Please do. Open-lab Saturday afternoons are exactly for this — drop in, watch a demo, interrogate the alumni. Or come to Family Build Day on 7 November 2026, which is free and finishes with cake.

Is my child too young / am I too old?

Kids Saturdays fits ages 9–15 comfortably. On the other end there is no end: our oldest graduate is 68 and automated her church’s bookkeeping. Curiosity is the entry requirement; birth year isn’t.

What happens after my cohort?

You keep building. Alumni get lifetime access to open-lab Saturdays, the community group, and first invitations to advanced sprints. The Lab is a door you walk through, not a course you finish.
